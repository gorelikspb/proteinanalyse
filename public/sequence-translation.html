DNA to Protein Translation Tool

Our free DNA translation tool converts DNA sequences to protein sequences using the standard genetic code. DNA to protein translation is a fundamental process in molecular biology, where nucleotide triplets (codons) are translated into amino acids according to the genetic code. This protein translation process is essential for understanding gene expression and protein synthesis.

The DNA translation tool reads your DNA sequence in the forward reading frame (Frame 1), converting each three-nucleotide codon into its corresponding amino acid. The tool uses the standard genetic code where 64 possible codons encode 20 standard amino acids and three stop codons. Use our main sequence analyzer for comprehensive analysis, ORF finder to identify coding regions before translation, or our protein molecular weight calculator to analyze the translated protein sequence.

All sequence translation is performed instantly in your browser with complete privacy. The DNA to protein translation tool is essential for bioinformatics researchers, molecular biologists, and students working on genome analysis, sequence analysis, and understanding the relationship between DNA sequences and their encoded proteins.
